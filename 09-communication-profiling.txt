COMMUNICATION PROFILING

Before the system can work well for you, your agent needs to understand how you think, talk, and organize. You can tell it directly, let it help you figure it out through conversation, or — if your tool supports it — have it analyze your existing interactions.

HOW TO BUILD YOUR PROFILE

There are three approaches. Pick whichever fits:

  1. Write it yourself. You already know how you like to work. Open your AGENTS.md and write an interaction style section: how verbose you want responses, whether you want suggestions or not, how you give instructions, what derails you.

  2. Co-create it with your agent. Start a conversation: "Help me figure out my communication preferences for this system." The agent asks you questions, you answer, and together you draft the profile. This is the recommended path if you're not sure what to write.

  3. Opt in to analysis. If your tool has access to your prior conversations and you're comfortable with it, you can ask the agent to scan your history for patterns. This is optional and only uses data already on your machine.

WHAT THE PROFILE CAPTURES

  How you give instructions: precise specs or broad intent? Do you iterate or expect first-pass accuracy?
  How you give feedback: do you explain why something's wrong or just redirect?
  How you think: lists, narratives, diagrams, compressed fragments? How much explanation do you want back?
  How you organize: by project, by urgency, by energy level, by domain?
  Your pace: do you batch instructions or stream them? Deep focus or frequent context-switches?
  Accessibility: ADHD, dyslexia, autism-spectrum, sensory preferences — anything affecting how info should be presented.
  Your tone: formal, casual, terse, playful, technical — or a blend that shifts by context.

You decide what to include. Nothing is extracted or written without your input.

THIS ISN'T A PERSONALITY TEST

It's calibration data. Your agent uses it to adjust response length, structure, vocabulary, detail level, when to ask vs. when to act, and how to format output.

Someone whose profile says "terse, compressed, ADHD, no preamble" gets fundamentally different agent behavior than someone whose profile says "detailed, exploratory, likes to think out loud."

WHERE IT GOES

Your communication profile is private by default. Two options for where to store it:

  Local only: Keep it in your machine's ~/AGENTS.md or in your tool's local config. Only agents on this machine see it. Use this if your profile contains personal or sensitive details (accessibility needs, cognitive style, etc.).

  Shared: Put it in the interaction style section of your global AGENTS.md. Every agent on every machine reads it. Use this if you want consistent behavior across all your tools and machines, and the content isn't sensitive.

You can split it — put general preferences (tone, verbosity, no-suggestions) in the shared file, and personal details (accessibility, cognitive patterns) in local config.

IT GETS BETTER OVER TIME

The first profile is a draft. It refines through use:

  When you correct your agent's behavior, update the profile to match
  When you notice patterns in how you work (you consistently shorten long responses, you skip certain types of output), add them
  You can edit the profile any time — it's just a section in a text file

After a few sessions, corrections should become rare.

YOUR ORGANIZATION STYLE TOO

Beyond communication, you might also want to capture how you organize:

  How you name things (files, branches, variables, projects)
  How you structure directories
  How you track tasks (formal system, scattered notes, or just your head)
  How you prioritize (deadlines, energy, interest, urgency)
  How you handle interruptions
  What "done" means to you

This shapes how the agent behaves during and after sessions — what it reports, what it tracks, what it leaves alone.

STARTING FROM ZERO

If you don't know your preferences yet, that's fine. Start with the basics — how verbose do you want responses? Do you want suggestions or not? — and add more as you notice what works and what doesn't. The profile grows with you.
